Don't sell unless you have to.

Compounding does the heavy lifting on a catch-up timeline, and every sale outside an IRA is a taxable event.

Rule 1
The Doubling Rule
One rule. One trigger. No charts.
Every time Bitcoin doubles from your last harvest price, sell enough to cover one or two years of living expenses. Then your next trigger doubles again. You never have to predict the top.
Enter your cost basis above to activate this rule.
Rule 4
Calendar Harvest
For people drawing down on Bitcoin
In any year you need income, you must sell something if Bitcoin spent any part of the year at least 50% above your cost basis. Removes the "what if it goes higher" trap.
Enter your cost basis above to activate this rule.
Rule 3
200-Week Moving Average / Mayer Multiple
Bitcoin only
The Mayer Multiple is today's price divided by its 200-week moving average (the long-term trend line). Historically every Bitcoin cycle top has appeared in the stretched-to-sell-zone bands. As Bitcoin has matured, peaks have been getting lower, so the thresholds below are tightened from the book's original numbers to reflect recent cycle behavior.

Rule 5
Ladder Selling Strategy
For altcoins, narrative stocks, exit-positions
For positions you've decided to fully exit, sell in tranches based on how many times your money you've made (the "multiple"). The exact ladder depends on the asset's risk tier. Stocks follow thesis-driven rules, not price-driven ones.
Low-cap crypto (high risk)
Sell 25% at 10x from your cost basis
Sell 25% at 15x
Sell 25% at 20x
Sell 25% at 25x or at clear cycle top
Blue-chip crypto (ETH, SOL, SUI)
Sell 25% at 5x from your cost basis
Sell 25% at 8x
Sell 25% at 12x
Hold or sell remainder at cycle top
Narrative stocks (NVDA, TSLA)
Thesis-driven, not price-driven. Sell when:
• The investment thesis materially breaks
• Valuation becomes extreme
• Position exceeds target % allocation
• In an IRA: rebalance freely (no tax event)
Reminder. These rules apply only to positions you've decided to fully exit. For positions you intend to keep (especially Bitcoin), the core thesis stands: don't sell unless you have to.
